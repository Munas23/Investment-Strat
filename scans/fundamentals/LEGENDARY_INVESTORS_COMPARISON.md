## Universal Metrics Across ALL Successful Investors

### **Top 5 Most Common Metrics**

1. **ROE / ROIC** (Return on Equity / Return on Invested Capital)
   - **Value Investors**: 15-20%+ (Buffett demands 20%+ 10-yr average)
   - **Growth Investors**: 17%+ (O'Neil, Minervini)
   - **Quantitative**: 25%+ (Greenblatt)
   - **WHY**: Measures how efficiently company uses capital

2. **Earnings Growth**
   - **Value**: 10%+ sustained, 33%+ over 10 years (Graham)
   - **GARP**: 15-25% sweet spot (Lynch)
   - **Growth**: 25-50%+ (O'Neil), 40-100%+ for best performers
   - **WHY**: Stock prices follow earnings over time

3. **Debt Levels**
   - **All Investors**: Debt/Equity <1.0 to <1.5
   - **Conservative**: Debt <2x earnings (Buffett <5x)
   - **WHY**: Financial flexibility, survives downturns

4. **Profit Margins**
   - **Buffett**: Gross margin >40%
   - **All**: Above industry average, preferably expanding
   - **WHY**: Pricing power, operating efficiency

5. **Competitive Moat / Quality**
   - **Buffett/Munger**: Durable competitive advantages
   - **Fisher**: Qualitative excellence (15-point checklist)
   - **WHY**: Sustainable profitability, long-term edge
