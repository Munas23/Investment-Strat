# Growth/Momentum Traders

## 1. Mark Minervini

### Overview
Mark Minervini is the creator of the SEPA (Specific Entry Point Analysis) methodology and won the U.S. Investing Championship with a 155% return. His approach combines fundamental strength with precise technical entry points.

### Primary Technical Setup: Volatility Contraction Pattern (VCP)

**Pattern Characteristics:**
- **Pullback Sequence**: 2-6 pullbacks with each successive pullback smaller than the previous
  - Example progression: 20% → 10% → 5%
  - Alternative example: 25% → 15% → 8%
  - Final pullback typically 5-10%
- **Volume Behavior**: Volume decreases progressively during each pullback
- **Line of Least Resistance**: Creates tightening price action moving left to right on chart
- **Prior Uptrend Required**: Stock must have established uptrend before VCP formation

### Trend Template (8 Exact Criteria)

A stock MUST meet ALL 8 criteria to be in a confirmed Stage 2 uptrend:

1. **Current Price Position**: Above both 150-day AND 200-day SMA
2. **150-Day MA Position**: Above the 200-day SMA
3. **200-Day MA Trend**: Trending up for minimum 1 month (preferably 4-5 months)
4. **50-Day MA Position**: Above both 150-day AND 200-day SMAs
5. **Price vs 50-Day MA**: Current price above 50-day SMA
6. **Distance from 52-Week Low**: At least 30% above 52-week low
7. **Distance from 52-Week High**: Within 25% of 52-week high (closer is better)
8. **Relative Strength**: RS Rating of 85 or higher (vs. 100)

### Entry Criteria

**Primary Entry - Breakout:**
- Wait for clean pivot point at top of tight consolidation
- Breakout must occur on explosive volume
- Strong relative strength vs. market on breakout day
- Entry within first 5% above breakout point
- Requires definable uptrend

**Alternative Entry - Pullback:**
- Must occur after valid breakout
- Pullback maximum 8-10% from breakout high
- Lower volume on pullback (healthy retracement indicator)
- Resume entry on volume expansion

### Volume Requirements
- Breakout volume significantly above average (explosive)
- Decreasing volume during VCP formation
- Volume expansion confirms strength on entry

### Key Indicators
- **Moving Averages**: 50-day, 150-day, 200-day (all simple)
- **Relative Strength Rating**: 85+ required (vs. all stocks)
- **Volume**: Comparative analysis vs. recent average

### Risk Management
- **Stop Loss**: 3-5% from entry (tight consolidation allows close stops)
- **Position Sizing**: Based on tight stop placement
- **Cut Losses Immediately**: If breaks below pivot/base

### Fundamental Criteria (Also Required)
- **EPS Growth**: 20-50%+ year-over-year
- **Revenue Growth**: Minimum 20%
- **Profit Margins**: Expanding
- **Institutional Ownership**: Increasing (accumulation)
- **RS Rating**: Above 70 minimum, 85+ ideal

### Timeframes
- **Primary Chart**: Daily for pattern identification
- **Context**: Weekly for overall trend confirmation
- **Entry Timing**: Intraday for precise execution

### Stage Analysis
Uses Weinstein Stage Analysis framework:
- Only buys Stage 2 stocks (advancing phase)
- Avoids Stage 1 (basing), Stage 3 (topping), Stage 4 (declining)

## 2. William O'Neil

### Overview
Creator of CAN SLIM methodology and founder of Investor's Business Daily. Developed the cup-with-handle pattern and institutional-grade stock screening systems.

### Primary Technical Setups

#### Setup #1: Cup-with-Handle Pattern

**Cup Formation:**
- **Shape**: U-shaped (rounded bottom preferred over V-shape)
- **Depth**: Ideally 1/3 or less of previous advance
  - Maximum acceptable: 1/2 of previous advance in volatile markets
- **Duration**: 7-65 weeks
  - Avoid bases less than 7 weeks (unreliable)
  - Avoid 1-3 week bases (too risky)
- **Prior Trend**: Minimum 30% advance from last intermediate low

**Handle Formation:**
- **Pullback Depth**: Maximum 1/3 of cup's advance
  - Typically 10-30% of cup height
- **Duration**: Shorter than cup (days to weeks)
- **Volume**: Declining volume during handle formation
- **Price Behavior**: Tight, low-volume pullback

**Entry Trigger:**
- Break above upper trendline of handle
- Breakout on strong volume (see volume requirements below)

#### Setup #2: Flat Base Pattern

**Characteristics:**
- **Duration**: Minimum 5-6 weeks
- **Price Movement**: Sideways trading action
- **Depth**: Maximum correction of 10-15%
- **Volume**: Generally lower during base formation

### Entry Criteria

**Aggressive Entry:**
- Buy stop order slightly above handle's upper trendline
- Entry at breakout confirmation

**Conservative Entry:**
- Wait for successful retest of breakout level
- Reduces false breakout risk
- Enter on bounce from support

### Volume Requirements

**Critical Thresholds:**
- **Minimum Breakout Volume**: 40% above average (1.4x relative volume)
- **Ideal Breakout Volume**: 50%+ above 50-day average
- **Premium Breakouts**: 100-200%+ above average volume
- **Handle Volume**: Declining/diminishing (essential)
- **Volume Confirmation**: Required for institutional buying confirmation

### Moving Averages & Indicators

**Key Moving Averages:**
- **Primary**: 50-day SMA
- **Context**: 200-day SMA
- Price should stay above SMAs during base formation
- Uptrend confirmation via MA positioning

**Relative Strength (RS) Rating:**
- **Minimum**: RS Rating above 80
- **Ideal**: RS Rating 90+
- **Calculation**: Percentile rank of 12-month price performance
  - 40% weight on recent 3 months
  - 20% weight on each of the prior 3 quarters
- **Historical Data**: Best performers averaged RS of 87 before major moves
- **Sell Signal**: RS falls below 50 (leadership lost)

### Risk Management
- **Stop Loss**: 7-8% below purchase price (hard rule)
- **Never average down losses**
- Cut losses quickly and let winners run

### Fundamental Criteria (CAN SLIM)

**C - Current Earnings:**
- Quarterly EPS up minimum 20-50% year-over-year

**A - Annual Earnings:**
- Annual earnings increase of 24%+ year-over-year

**N - New:**
- New product, new management, new highs
- Price making new 52-week highs

**S - Supply & Demand:**
- Shares outstanding consideration
- Institutional ownership increasing

**L - Leader:**
- RS Rating 80+ (leaders only)
- Avoid laggards

**I - Institutional Sponsorship:**
- Minimum quality institutional ownership
- Increasing ownership preferred

**M - Market Direction:**
- Trade with market trend
- Most important factor (3 out of 4 stocks follow market)

### Timeframes
- **Primary**: Weekly charts for base identification
- **Daily**: For entry timing and handle formation
- **Entry**: Can use intraday for precise execution

### Specific Thresholds Summary
- Base duration: 7-65 weeks
- Cup depth: 12-33%
- Handle depth: 10-30% of cup
- Breakout volume: 1.4x-2x average minimum
- RS Rating: 80+ (90+ ideal)
- Stop loss: 7-8% maximum
- Entry point: Within 5% of breakout ideal

## 3. Nicolas Darvas

### Overview
Self-taught dancer turned investor who made $2 million in the stock market using his "Box Theory." Traded while touring, relying purely on price and volume.

### Primary Technical Setup: Darvas Box

**Box Formation Rules:**

1. **Upper Boundary (Resistance)**:
   - Price makes new high
   - Subsequent price action fails to exceed this high (tested 3+ times)
   - This high becomes the box top

2. **Lower Boundary (Support)**:
   - After making high, price retraces
   - Low point established where price finds support
   - This low becomes the box bottom

3. **Box Confirmation**:
   - Price oscillates between these boundaries
   - Box is "confirmed" when boundaries are tested and hold
   - New box forms when price breaks out and establishes new range

### Entry Criteria

**Exact Entry Rules:**

1. **Breakout Trigger**:
   - Price closes above the upper boundary (box top)
   - Entry immediately upon breakout above resistance

2. **Volume Confirmation**:
   - Breakout must occur with increased trading volume
   - Shows genuine buying pressure vs. manipulation

3. **Trend Requirement**:
   - Only take long positions in confirmed uptrends
   - Stock must be making new highs (rising boxes)
   - Each new box should be higher than previous box

4. **Already Rising**:
   - Best time to buy is when stock already rising
   - Breakout from consolidation to new highs ideal

### Volume Requirements

**Critical Importance:**
- Volume is equal to price in importance
- Increased volume confirms breakout validity
- Volume shows strength of buying/selling pressure
- Breakout + high volume = valid entry signal
- Breakout + low volume = suspect (avoid)

### Exit Criteria

**Exit Rules:**

1. **Box Breakdown**:
   - Sell immediately if price closes below box bottom (lower boundary)
   - This is the primary stop-loss mechanism

2. **Failure to Form New Box**:
   - If price fails to create higher box after breakout
   - If price breaks back into previous box after breakout attempt

3. **Trailing Stop System**:
   - As new boxes form (higher highs), raise stop-loss
   - Stop is always bottom of most recent box
   - Progressive elevation of stops locks in profits

4. **Signs of Weakness**:
   - Stock reaching new high then falling back into box
   - Breaking through previous box's lower boundary

### Risk Management

**Stop Loss Methodology:**
- **Initial Stop**: Bottom of breakout box
- **Trailing Stops**: Progressively raise to bottom of each new box
- **Hard Exit**: Immediate sell if stop violated
- **No Second Guessing**: Systematic rule following required

### Fundamental Filters (Also Used)

While primarily technical, Darvas did screen for:
- **Sector Selection**: High excitement potential industries
- **Revolutionary Products**: Companies creating new products
- **Earnings Growth**: Healthy earnings over time
- **Growth Sectors**: Leading edge industries

### Timeframes
- **Primary**: Daily charts for box identification
- **Entry/Exit**: End of day for decision making
- **Monitoring**: Daily close to identify box boundaries

### Key Indicators
- **Price**: Primary indicator (box boundaries)
- **Volume**: Confirmation indicator
- **No traditional indicators**: Pure price/volume approach

### Specific Thresholds
- Box confirmation: 3+ tests of boundary
- Breakout: Close above resistance
- Volume: "Increased" (vs. average, specific % not defined)
- Stop: Bottom of current box (mechanical)

### Stage Analysis
- Implied Stage 2 only (advancing stocks with rising boxes)
- Avoids declining trends (no short positions in his method)

## 4. Jesse Livermore

### Overview
Legendary speculator known as the "Great Bear of Wall Street." Made and lost several fortunes. Pioneer of tape reading and pivotal point trading in early 1900s.

### Primary Technical Setup: Pivotal Points

**Pivotal Point Definition:**

Pivotal Points are critical price levels:

1. **Previous Reversal Points**:
   - Major previous tops
   - Major previous bottoms
   - Historical significant support/resistance

2. **Psychological Levels**:
   - Round numbers: 50, 100, 200, etc.
   - Whole dollar amounts
   - Significant psychological barriers

### Entry Criteria - Breakout Confirmation Pattern

**Phase 1: Initial Move**
- Unusually large volume at start of move
- Price breaks above/below pivotal point
- Volume must be exceptional (well above normal)

**Phase 2: Directional Movement**
- Prices move in one direction for several days
- Uptrend: series of higher prices
- Downtrend: series of lower prices

**Phase 3: Normal Reaction**
- Temporary pause or retracement
- **Volume decreases** significantly vs. initial move
- Price may move slightly against trend
- Duration: 1-2 days typically
- This is healthy, expected behavior

**Phase 4: Trend Resumption**
- Within 1-2 days after normal reaction
- Volume increases again
- Price trend resumes in original direction
- Confirms validity of initial breakout

### Trading Rules

**Core Rules:**

1. **Coordinate with Pivot Points**:
   - Only enter after market confirms opinion
   - Wait for pivotal point breakout

2. **Prompt Entry**:
   - Enter immediately upon confirmation
   - Don't hesitate once pattern confirmed

3. **Position Management**:
   - Continue trades showing profit
   - End trades showing loss immediately

4. **Trend Identification**:
   - Exit when trend clearly over
   - Don't fight the tape

5. **Never Average Down**:
   - Adding to losing positions forbidden
   - Only pyramid into winners

### Volume Requirements

**Initial Breakout:**
- Unusually large volume required
- Well above normal trading activity
- Indicates institutional participation

**Normal Reaction:**
- Volume must decrease substantially
- Shows selling pressure exhausted
- Lower volume confirms healthy pullback

**Resumption:**
- Volume expansion required
- Confirms renewed buying/selling interest
- Validates trend continuation

### Key Indicators

**Tape Reading Elements:**
- **Price Action**: Real-time price changes
- **Volume**: Size and speed of transactions
- **Momentum**: Rate of price change
- **Psychological Price Levels**: Round numbers, previous pivots

**Analysis Focus:**
- Speed of price changes
- Size of transactions
- Market sentiment (bulls vs. bears)
- Institutional player activity

### Timeframes
- **Primary**: Daily charts for pivot identification
- **Real-time**: Intraday tape reading (when active)
- **Execution**: Immediate upon confirmation

### Risk Management
- Cut losses quickly
- Let profits run (takes time to make big money)
- Never average down
- Exit when trend breaks

### Trading Philosophy

**Key Principles:**

1. **Patience**: Trade only when real opportunities exist
2. **Pattern Recognition**: Markets repeat historically
3. **Trend Following**: Follow the trend direction
4. **Time**: Big money takes time to develop
5. **Confirmation**: Wait for market to prove you right

### Specific Thresholds
- Pivotal points: Previous major highs/lows, psychological levels (50, 100, 200)
- Volume: "Unusually large" on breakout, "decreased" on reaction, "increased" on resumption
- Normal reaction: 1-2 days duration
- Entry: Immediately upon confirmation (Phase 4)

### Stage Analysis
- Focus on breakouts from consolidation
- Only trade when market confirms direction
- Primarily Stage 2 (advancing) focus for longs

**Critical Philosophy:**
- Pattern alone does NOT guarantee success
- Must combine with volume + fundamentals + sector strength
- Context is everything

### Entry Criteria

**Technical Entry Rules:**

1. **Breakout from Good Base**:
   - Must be well-formed base or pivot area
   - Wait for leading stocks to break out
   - Powerful volume required (see below)

   - **Never enter more than 5% above technical buy point**
   - This is a hard rule
   - Reduces risk if trade fails

3. **Exit Quickly on Failure**:
   - If stock falls under buy point, exit immediately

4. **Group/Sector Strength**:
   - Must pay attention to sector strength

**Exact Criteria:**

2. **Volume = Institutional Buying**:
   - High volume indicates institutional participation

3. **Volume as Confirmation**:
   - Volume is "the market's heartbeat"

### Fundamental Criteria

**Earnings Requirements:**

4. **Return on Equity (ROE)**:
   - Minimum: 17%+ ROE

### Key Indicators

**Zanger Uses ZERO Traditional Indicators:**

- No Stochastics
- No Oscillators

**Only Uses:**
1. **Price**: Chart patterns only
2. **Volume**: 20-day MA as baseline
3. **Fundamentals**: Earnings/sales growth

### Risk Management

**Position Sizing:**
- **Never risk more than 1% of portfolio per trade**
- Ensures no single loss significantly impacts capital
- Allows for many attempts to find winners

**Stop Loss:**
- **8% rule**: Sell if stock falls 8% below purchase price
- No questions asked
- Immediate exit, no hoping

### Timeframes
- **Primary**: Daily charts for pattern identification
- **Intraday**: Monitors price/volume action during day
- **Entry**: Breakout confirmation on daily close ideal

### Specific Thresholds Summary
- Entry distance: Maximum 5% above buy point
- Volume minimum: 50-300% above 20-day average
- EPS growth: 40-50% minimum year-over-year
- Quarterly EPS: 40%+ minimum
- Quarterly sales: 40%+ minimum
- ROE: 17%+ for 3 years
- Stop loss: 8% below entry
- Position risk: Maximum 1% of portfolio

### Stage Analysis
- Focuses on Stage 2 (advancing) stocks
- Only buys leaders breaking out
- Avoids weak patterns and laggards

# Trend Followers

## 1. Richard Dennis & Turtle Traders

### Overview
Richard Dennis proved that trading could be taught through the famous Turtle Trading experiment. Turned $1,600 into $200 million. The Turtles were taught a complete rules-based breakout system.

### Primary Technical Setup: Donchian Channel Breakouts

The Turtle system uses two distinct variations:

#### System 1: Short-Term (20-Day Breakout)

**Entry Rules:**
- **Long Entry**: Buy when price hits 20-day high
- **Short Entry**: Sell when price hits 20-day low

**Special Filter Rule:**
- Skip the breakout signal if the last signal was profitable
- Direction irrelevant (losing long OR losing short enables next trade)
- If skipped, use backup entry (see below)

**Backup Entry:**
- If System 1 signal skipped (previous winner)
- Enter at **55-day breakout** instead
- Ensures you don't miss major moves

#### System 2: Long-Term (55-Day Breakout)

**Entry Rules:**
- **Long Entry**: Buy when price hits 55-day high
- **Short Entry**: Sell when price hits 55-day low
- **No filter**: All signals taken

### Position Sizing - The "Unit" System

**N Definition (Volatility Measure):**
- N = 20-day exponential moving average of True Range
- True Range = Daily range accounting for gaps
- Also called ATR (Average True Range)

**Unit Calculation:**

```
Unit = (Account Equity × 0.01) / (N × Dollar Value Per Point)
```

**Translation:**
- 1 Unit sized so 1 N move = 1% of account
- Maximum risk per trade: 2% of account
- Build positions in Units (see pyramiding below)

**Practical Example:**
- Account: $100,000
- N: $1.00
- Unit Size: $1,000 risk
- Position sized so $1.00 move = $1,000 loss/gain

### Pyramiding Rules

**Adding to Positions:**
- Enter initial position: 1 Unit (0.5% risk)
- Add Units at **1/2 N (1/2 ATR) intervals**
- Continue adding as trade moves in favor
- Maximum total position: 2% account risk

**Example Pyramid:**
- Entry: 0.5% position
- +1/2 ATR: Add 0.5% (now 1.0% total)
- +1 ATR: Add 0.5% (now 1.5% total)
- +1.5 ATR: Add 0.5% (now 2.0% total - maximum)

### Stop Loss Rules

**Initial Stop:**
- **Long positions**: Entry price - 2 ATR (2N)
- **Short positions**: Entry price + 2 ATR (2N)
- Hard stop, placed at entry
- Represents 2% maximum account loss

**Trailing Stop:**
- Stops adjusted as new Units added
- Always maintain 2 ATR stop from current price
- Protects profits as position builds

### Exit Rules

**System 1 Exits:**
- Long positions: Exit on 10-day low
- Short positions: Exit on 10-day high

**System 2 Exits:**
- Long positions: Exit on 20-day low
- Short positions: Exit on 20-day high

### Allocation Between Systems
- Traders could split equity between System 1 and System 2
- Common allocation: 50/50 split
- Personal preference allowed

### Volume Requirements
- Not a primary factor in Turtle system
- Focused on price breakouts only
- Liquidity required for position sizing (futures/commodities)

### Key Indicators
- **Primary**: Donchian Channels (highest high/lowest low)
- **Risk Measurement**: 20-day ATR (N)
- **No other indicators used**

### Timeframes
- **Exclusively daily timeframe**
- System 1: 20-day lookback
- System 2: 55-day lookback
- End-of-day signals only

### Market Selection
- Originally commodities/futures
- Diversified across multiple uncorrelated markets
- Required sufficient liquidity for position sizing

### Risk Management Philosophy
- **Maximum single trade risk**: 2% of account
- **Unit-based position sizing**: Equalizes volatility across markets
- **Pyramiding**: Builds winners, not losers
- **Hard stops**: No discretion, mechanical exit

### Specific Thresholds Summary
- System 1 entry: 20-day high/low
- System 2 entry: 55-day high/low
- System 1 exit: 10-day high/low
- System 2 exit: 20-day high/low
- ATR period: 20 days (exponential MA)
- Stop distance: 2 ATR from entry
- Pyramid interval: 0.5 ATR
- Position risk: 2% maximum
- Unit risk: 1% of account per 1 ATR move

### Stage Analysis
- System is trend-following, not stage-based
- Enters breakouts regardless of prior stage
- Rides trends until reversal signal

## 2. Ed Seykota

### Overview
Pioneer of systematic/computerized trading in the 1970s. Turned $5,000 into $15 million. Champion of trend following and emotional discipline. Created one of first computerized trend-following systems.

### Primary Technical Setup: Trend Following

**Core Philosophy:**
- "The trend is your friend"
- Ride prevailing market trends
- Don't predict, react to price
- Simplicity over complexity

### Key Technical Indicators

**Moving Averages:**
- **Type**: Exponential Moving Averages (EMAs)
- Gives more weight to recent data
- Primary trend identification tool
- Specific periods not publicly disclosed (proprietary)

**Momentum Oscillators:**
- Determine trend strength
- Identify potential reversal points
- Used as confirmation, not primary signal

**Volume Indicators:**
- Insight into trend intensity
- Likelihood of trend continuation
- Confirmation of price moves

### Trading Rules - "The Five Rules"

1. **Cut Losses**
   - Exit losing trades quickly
   - Don't let small losses become big losses

2. **Ride Winners**
   - Let profitable trades run
   - Don't exit prematurely
   - Trend may continue longer than expected

3. **Keep Bets Small**
   - Conservative position sizing
   - Survive to trade another day

4. **Follow the Rules Without Question**
   - Systematic adherence required
   - No discretionary overrides
   - Emotional discipline essential

5. **Know When to Break the Rules**
   - Advanced concept
   - Requires deep experience
   - Rarely applicable

### Entry Criteria

**Trend Identification:**
- Price above/below exponential moving averages
- Momentum oscillators confirm direction
- Volume supports move

**Entry Timing:**
- End-of-day approach
- Reduces premature exits
- Daily chart focus (not intraday)

**Breakout Strategy:**
- Simple moving average crossovers
- Price breakouts from consolidation
- Clear, repeatable patterns

### Volume Requirements
- Higher volume confirms trend strength
- Volume expansion = continuation likely
- Volume contraction = trend weakening possible

### Risk Management

**Position Sizing:**
- **Speculate with less than 10% of liquid net worth**
- Conservative approach to preserve capital
- Multiple positions across markets

**Stop Losses:**
- Based on systematic rules
- Not discretionary
- Protects against catastrophic loss

### Timeframes
- **Primary**: Daily charts (end-of-day data)
- Avoids intraday noise
- Easier trend identification
- Promotes patience

### Key Indicators Recap
- Exponential Moving Averages (primary)
- Momentum Oscillators (confirmation)
- Volume Indicators (confirmation)
- Simple, clear signals

### Trading Philosophy

**Systematic Approach:**
- Rules-based system
- Removes emotion from trading
- Computerized execution (pioneered this)
- Consistent application

**Simplicity:**
- Complexity doesn't improve results
- Straightforward indicators
- Clear entry/exit rules
- Easy to execute

**Emotional Discipline:**
- Psychology is critical
- Follow system regardless of feelings

# Stage Analysis Framework

## Stan Weinstein's Four Stages

Many successful traders use stage analysis to filter stocks. Here's the complete framework:

### Stage 1: Basing/Accumulation

**Characteristics:**
- Price moves sideways after Stage 4 decline
- Supply and demand reach equilibrium
- Price oscillates above and below 30-week MA
- Base formation (horizontal range)

**30-Week MA Behavior:**
- Flattens out following price horizontally
- No longer declining
- Not yet rising

**Volume:**
- Typically low
- Selling pressure diminished
- Accumulation beginning

**Trading Action:**
- **DO NOT BUY in Stage 1**
- Wait for Stage 2 breakout
- Identify for watchlist

### Stage 2: Advancing Phase (BUY ZONE)

**Characteristics:**
- Price breaks above Stage 1 trading range
- Moves above key moving averages (30-week, 200-day)
- Makes higher highs AND higher lows
- If no higher lows, it's NOT Stage 2

**30-Week MA Behavior:**
- Climbing upward
- Price remains above MA
- MA trails price

**Volume:**
- Breakout volume 2-3x average (ideal)
- Significant expansion on breakout
- Confirms institutional buying

**Trading Action:**
- **PRIMARY BUYING ZONE**
- Highest probability long trades
- All growth/momentum traders focus here
- Hold until Stage 3 appears

### Stage 3: Top/Distribution

**Characteristics:**
- Price levels out, moves horizontally
- Forms defined trading range
- Sharp peaks and troughs
- Choppy, difficult price action

**30-Week MA Behavior:**
- Still climbing but flattening
- Catches up to price
- Eventually slices through price

**Volume:**
- Heavy volume on both up AND down days
- Shows distribution
- Buyers and sellers balanced
- Churning action

**Trading Action:**
- **EXIT LONGS**
- Do not buy new positions
- Market struggling, sellers gaining control
- Prepare for Stage 4

### Stage 4: Declining Phase

**Characteristics:**
- Price breaks down from Stage 3 top
- May pullback into range briefly
- Then continues downward
- Lower highs and lower lows

**30-Week MA Behavior:**
- Remains above price
- Price trades below MA
- MA may begin declining

**Volume:**
- Can be heavy or light
- Panic selling creates volume spikes
- Steady decline possible on lower volume

**Trading Action:**
- **DO NOT BUY**
- Short opportunities for experienced traders
- Avoid completely for long-only traders
- Wait for Stage 1 base to form

### Key Moving Average: 30-Week MA

**Why 30-Week:**
- Approximately 150-day MA
- Smooths out noise
- Identifies major trend
- Used on weekly charts

**Application:**
- Plot on weekly chart
- Compare price position relative to MA
- Observe MA slope (rising/falling/flat)
- Combine with price action

### Using Stage Analysis

**Stock Selection:**
- Only buy Stage 2 stocks (advancing)
- Avoid all other stages for longs
- Dramatically improves win rate

**Combination with Other Methods:**
- Minervini: Stage 2 + Trend Template + VCP
- O'Neil: Stage 2 + CAN SLIM + Cup and Handle
- Darvas: Stage 2 (rising boxes only)
- Zanger: Stage 2 breakouts with volume

**Weekly Chart Focus:**
- Stage analysis primarily on weekly
- Daily for entry timing
- Weekly for stage identification

### Specific Criteria Summary

**Stage 1:**
- Price: Oscillating around 30-week MA
- MA: Flat
- Volume: Low
- Action: Wait

**Stage 2:**
- Price: Above 30-week MA, making higher highs/lows
- MA: Rising, below price
- Volume: 2-3x average on breakout
- Action: BUY

**Stage 3:**
- Price: Choppy around 30-week MA
- MA: Flattening, slicing through price
- Volume: Heavy both directions
- Action: SELL/AVOID

**Stage 4:**
- Price: Below 30-week MA, making lower lows
- MA: Above price, may be declining
- Volume: Variable
- Action: AVOID (or SHORT for advanced)
